- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project is at a high-potential stage where the "Publishability Delta" is significant. The results already demonstrate that the 21 cm forest provides a unique, high-sensitivity probe of DM-baryon scattering that is orders of magnitude more stringent than existing CMB or global 21 cm constraints. However, the current analysis relies on assumptions (subhalo boost consistency, $v_{rel}$ degeneracy, and the validity of the linear transfer function at extreme suppression) that are standard targets for rigorous peer review. 

The proposed changes—specifically the "Stress Test" on subhalo model uncertainty, the explicit degeneracy contour plots for $M_{cut}$ vs. $J_{21}$, and the comparative analysis with Lyman-$\alpha$ forest mass scales—are not merely "technical layers" but are essential to solidify the scientific claim of "uniqueness." These improvements move the manuscript from a "promising simulation study" to a "definitive cosmological constraint paper." The ROI of effort is high because the data is already computed; the remaining work is analytical refinement and robust statistical validation. Given the potential to claim a 4-5 order-of-magnitude improvement over SOTA, the effort required to address these reviewer-critical points is well-justified and will significantly increase the likelihood of acceptance in a top-tier journal.
