**Title: Breaking the $M_{cut}-J_{21}$ Degeneracy via Redshift-Dependent Spectral Curvature Analysis**

**Hypothesis**: The degeneracy between DM-induced structure suppression ($M_{cut}$) and astrophysical photoheating ($J_{21}$) can be broken by exploiting the distinct redshift evolution of their respective signatures in the 21 cm forest. While both parameters suppress the number of absorbers, $M_{cut}$ acts as a sharp, mass-dependent cutoff in the halo mass function that evolves primarily with the growth of structure, whereas $J_{21}$ (Jeans filtering) acts as a smooth, time-integrated thermal pressure effect. 

**Proposed Method**: Instead of treating $z=10$ as a static optimal window, we will perform a joint Fisher analysis across the full redshift range $z \in [7, 15]$. We hypothesize that the "spectral curvature" of the $dN/d\tau$ distribution—specifically the ratio of the slope at low-$\tau$ vs. high-$\tau$—evolves differently for $M_{cut}$ and $J_{21}$ as the IGM transitions through reionization. By constructing a multi-redshift Fisher matrix that includes the cross-correlation of $\tau$-bins between different redshifts, we can isolate the $M_{cut}$ signal. We will test if this "redshift-lever" approach reduces the marginalized uncertainty on $M_{cut}$ by at least 30% compared to the single-redshift $z=10$ analysis, effectively disentangling the cosmological scattering signal from the astrophysical nuisance parameters.
