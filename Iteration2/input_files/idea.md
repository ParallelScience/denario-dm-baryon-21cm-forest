**Title**: Optimal Redshift and Optical Depth Sensitivity Mapping for DM-Baryon Scattering in the 21 cm Forest

**Description**: This study quantifies the sensitivity of the 21 cm forest to DM-baryon scattering by performing a derivative analysis of the cumulative optical depth distribution, $\partial N(>\tau) / \partial M_{cut}$, using the HAYASHI framework. We utilize the `cumulvec` data across $z=7–15$ to identify the specific $\tau$-bins that maximize the signal-to-noise ratio for detecting small-scale structure suppression. By mapping these sensitive bins to $\sigma_0/m_\chi$, we determine that $z \sim 10$ provides the optimal observational window where the cumulative suppression is most detectable against Poisson noise. We demonstrate that the sensitivity is driven by the depletion of low-mass minihalos, and we provide a optimized weighting scheme for SKA-era line-of-sight observations that prioritizes specific $\tau$ ranges to maximize constraints on the scattering cross-section. This approach moves beyond total absorber counts to establish a rigorous, data-driven observational strategy for distinguishing DM-baryon interaction models from CDM.
