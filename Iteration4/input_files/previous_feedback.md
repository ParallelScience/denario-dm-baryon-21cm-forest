The current analysis provides a robust, high-level demonstration of the 21 cm forest's potential to constrain DM-baryon scattering. However, to elevate this from a "proof-of-concept" to a rigorous scientific study suitable for publication, the following critical gaps must be addressed:

1. **Address the "Subhalo Boost" Circularity**: The analysis relies on the HAYASHI framework, which incorporates a subhalo boost model (Villanueva-Domingo et al. 2023). The current results assume this boost is independent of $M_{cut}$. However, if $M_{cut}$ suppresses the formation of host halos, it must logically suppress the formation of subhalos within those hosts. You must verify if HAYASHI self-consistently scales the subhalo contribution with $M_{cut}$. If it does not, your sensitivity estimates are likely over-optimistic, as the "subhalo boost" may be artificially inflating the signal in suppressed models.

2. **Refine the Fisher Forecast (Astrophysical Degeneracy)**: The current Fisher matrix assumes $J_{21}$ and $\bar{x}_{HI}$ are nuisance parameters with Gaussian priors. This is insufficient. The 21 cm forest is highly sensitive to the *spatial* distribution of ionizing sources. You must explicitly test if a "patchy" reionization model (which introduces spatial variance in $J_{21}$) mimics the $M_{cut}$ signal. If the "shape-based discriminant" (the $dN/d\tau$ slope) is the primary tool to break this degeneracy, you must demonstrate that this slope is invariant to the *topology* of reionization, not just the *mean* value of $J_{21}$.

3. **Clarify the $v_{rel}$ Assumption**: The analysis mentions comparing against a "no-streaming" model, but the impact of the relative velocity ($v_{rel}$) between DM and baryons is a known source of small-scale suppression itself (Shimabukuro et al. 2023). You must explicitly state whether your $M_{cut}$ parameterization *includes* or *is in addition to* the $v_{rel}$ effect. If they are additive, the degeneracy between $v_{rel}$ and $M_{cut}$ must be quantified; otherwise, your cross-section constraints are potentially biased by the omission of the kinematic streaming effect.

4. **Actionable Improvement for Future Iterations**:
    - **Sensitivity Mapping**: Instead of just reporting $\sigma(M_{cut})$, provide a "Degeneracy Plot" (contour plots of $M_{cut}$ vs. $J_{21}$ and $M_{cut}$ vs. $\bar{x}_{HI}$). This is essential for a reviewer to see that the "shape-based discriminant" actually works.
    - **Minimum Analysis Requirement**: You do not need more simulations. Instead, perform a "Stress Test": re-run the Fisher forecast with a 50% larger uncertainty on the subhalo contribution model. If the $M_{cut}$ constraints hold, the result is robust. If they collapse, the paper must frame the results as "conditional on subhalo modeling."
    - **Physical Interpretation**: The $n=-4$ cross-section sensitivity ($10^{-43}$ cm$^2$/GeV) is extremely low. Ensure you explicitly discuss the validity of the semianalytic approximation at these scales—specifically, whether the linear transfer function $T(M)$ remains valid when the scattering is so strong that it potentially alters the halo formation epoch significantly.

5. **Big Picture**: The claim that the 21 cm forest is "uniquely powerful" is strong. To support this, you must explicitly compare the $M_{cut}$ sensitivity to the *Lyman-$\alpha$ forest* using the same units. If Lyman-$\alpha$ is sensitive to $M_{cut} \sim 10^8 M_\odot$, clearly state that the 21 cm forest probes a regime (the "missing" $10^4-10^6 M_\odot$ range) that is physically inaccessible to any other current or planned probe.
